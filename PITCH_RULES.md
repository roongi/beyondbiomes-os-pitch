# Beyond Biomes — Rules for the Perfect 3-Minute Investor Pitch

Working document. Synthesized from: Salvatore workshop (general pitch craft), 4 Prem feedback audios + WhatsApp chat (Beyond-Biomes-specific), and a comparison of the two competing scripts (OS-first vs. soil-first). The next step builds the actual script from Section C.

---

## Section A — General pitch rules (from the Salvatore workshop)

Prioritized. Each rule has a one-line "why."

1. **First sentence must be instantly clear — it's your only hook.** *Why:* "This is your first touch point with the audience. If you don't get them here, then you lose them." Practice the opening line many times; it decides whether they keep listening.

2. **Open on the PROBLEM and make the audience feel how big/painful it is.** *Why:* "We always start from the problem... how big this problem is, how painful it is... If they don't understand how important the problem is, then they will not be able to also understand how important your solution is." Lights only go on when they grasp the pain.

3. **Move abstract → physical/tangible: problem → solution → product → market → traction.** *Why:* Salvatore's core arc is "bringing something from very, very abstract to something physical possible." Each beat should feel more concrete and more believable than the last.

4. **Reduce the message: explain it to a five-year-old.** *Why:* "Try to bring it to the five-year-old in the audience and don't overdo." A general/jury audience does not share your technical context. Simple-but-imprecise beats precise-but-incomprehensible.

5. **You don't have to state every limitation or caveat.** *Why:* "You don't have to say exactly what the limitations are... once you get them into a room... then you can talk about limitations. But what is it that you solve?" Caveats are for the follow-up meeting, not the pitch.

6. **Read your audience and frame for them.** *Why:* "First point is always read your audience." For this jury (VC, Volksbank MD, lawyer, Bryck — generalists who "understand how business works" but not your domain) the message is about the money and the business model, not app features.

7. **Don't over-index on product features/technical depth for investors.** *Why:* "Many focus a lot on the product, too much on the details... you will lose them... 'I wanna know about the money, not about the feature that you have in the app.'"

8. **De-risk: show team + traction.** *Why:* Investors are reading risk. "How do you de-risk a project in such an early phase... that comes through the team, it comes through the traction." Experience counts as traction: "I consider traction as team... you are part of the traction that you have."

9. **Use a personal/origin story if you have one — stories move emotion.** *Why:* "Stories is how we communicate... that's something that can always move emotions." A "warm recommendation."

10. **Always finish with an ASK.** *Why:* "Always finish with an ask... regardless of where you're going, always ask for something." A pitch without a concrete ask wastes the close.

11. **3 minutes = 3–5 slides max, ~1 minute per slide, light slides.** *Why:* "Three to five slides max... one minute per slide." Slides support you; they don't replace you. "The least stuff you have on there, the more attention you have on you."

12. **Don't be artsy; be tidy, clean, high-contrast, readable from the back.** *Why:* Salvatore repeatedly flags dark/over-designed slides as a failure. "Don't be artsy. So be tidy and clean, clear."

13. **The goal of 3 minutes is basic understanding + a reason to come talk to you.** *Why:* "You will never be able to convey everything... the goal is to deliver basic understanding... and give them the chance to get back to you." Make them more interested, not fully informed.

14. **One-sentence pitch as the baseline, then expand it.** *Why:* "A great start for a three-minute pitch is always to start with your one-sentence pitch, take it as baseline, and then go deeper." Formula: *[Company] develops [product] to help [customer] solve [problem] using [secret sauce].*

15. **Name the secret sauce — say WHY you're different, not just WHAT you do.** *Why:* Salvatore repeatedly pushed founders: "You want to say why you're different... what is the special thing about you." Years-of-experience alone is not a differentiator.

16. **Delivery is an incremental edge on top of content (not a replacement).** *Why:* "Do focus on your content... with time you can always do incremental improvements that don't cost you anything." Stable wide stance, weight centered, open palms, shoulders down; intentional gestures; look at people (or the back wall); use pauses to highlight the most important line; avoid caffeine before pitching.

## Section B — Beyond-Biomes-specific feedback (4 audios + WhatsApp chat)

What confused people, and what landed. Verbatim where it matters.

### B1. The "1000× operator" / OS-first opening is NOT understood — the central failure
- Ringo's review (WhatsApp): *"The first slide was very tough for me to understand. . 1000 times operator. . I could not understand this. 2. But as I scroll down, I was able to understand more."*
- Prem's wife (DLR project manager), audio 19-45-58: *"So beyond biomes, I do not understand what this company is about... Is it an operating system with the power of thousand operators? Or does it read soil? Or does it let one person run the entire company? What, what is going on? I don't understand."*
- Prem's wife, audio 19-52-13: *"I do not understand the meaning of operators, not passengers."*

**Takeaway:** The abstract "OS / 1000× operator" frame fails Rule A1 (first sentence must be clear) and A2 (graspable problem first). It collapses on slide 1. People only "got it" once soil appeared further down — direct evidence for the soil-first hypothesis.

### B2. "Is it an OS, a soil reader, or a company-runner?" — three competing identities on slide 1
- Audio 19-45-58: *"is it an operating system or a living soil reader?... So is it like a software that runs the company? Like what does it do?"*
- WhatsApp (Prem): *"Business Model is Vague (Not so clear)... Which of these are we asking funding for? ... But to investor, this is very confusing. We would need their 3 hours of undivided attention to convey this message."*

**Takeaway:** Pick ONE identity for the opening. The pitch must answer the investor's first question, which Prem names explicitly: *"The investor comes with the first expectations: 'Where does my money goes now?' And we answer this on very first slide very clearly. The 'agriculture' vertical."*

### B3. "5 verticals across 3 continents" reads as overreach / unfocused — actively harmful
- Audio 19-52-13: *"there are five verticals across three continents. That would make the funders think that you already have many, many products... if you are spread across three continents already... Is it a startup? Is it— what is going on? Have you already raised some money already?"*
- Audio 19-52-45: *"Investors will look for one or two verticals, and they will try to see whether you're focused or not. If you try to tread waters in all the verticals altogether... you are trying to go everywhere, and then you would end up being nowhere."*

**Takeaway:** Lead with ONE focused vertical (soil). The federation/multiple verticals is a *reveal at the end as upside*, not a front-loaded claim. Aligns with workshop Rule A6/A8.

### B4. The construction "already generating significant revenue" claim is fabricated — cut it
- WhatsApp (Ringo, re: the construction-revenue paragraph): *"this is really halluzinations but I take all into the improvement"* and *"yah but this is taste not evidence."*

**Takeaway:** Do NOT claim construction revenue. It's not real. Honesty + focus both point to dropping it from the 3-min pitch. (This kills a core beat of the soil-first script, Section 4 — see C notes.)

### B5. Slide-design failures echo the workshop verbatim
- Audio 19-45-58: *"your names are very small, like the letters are very tiny... I don't see your name."*
- Audio 19-48-01: *"the title itself is so small, I cannot see it. Eyes before muscle should be your tagline... the title matters... it's very tiny bullet points. I don't see anything, and I don't want to read so much text."* And: *"I think it goes for all the slides that you have."*

**Takeaway:** Big bold titles, almost no body text, visible team names/logo. Matches Rules A11/A12.

### B6. What the jury actually cares about (from Christiane, the organizer)
- WhatsApp (Christiane): *"in der Jury sitzen Vertreter aus dem Ökosystem, da geht's in erster Linie um das Geschäftsmodell nicht um das EXIST-Projekt. Anzahl Teammitglieder ist nicht entscheidend, gerne das aktuelle Konzept vorstellen."* (The jury is ecosystem reps; it's primarily about the BUSINESS MODEL, not the EXIST project; team size is not decisive — present the current concept.)
- Jury composition: Bochum Wirtschaftsentwicklung, VC, MD of Volksbank Sprockhövel, Aulinger (law firm, active in startups), Bryck.

**Takeaway:** Generalist business/finance audience. Lead with business model + money. The banker and VC need crisp numbers and a clear "where the money goes." De-emphasize team headcount.

### B7. What LANDED
- The soil-first structure reads clearly and has a built-in call to action. Prem: *"In my slide — the soil first 'vertical' approach, directly has a clear 'CALL TO ACTION' for the pitch."* Ringo agreed it *"goes in the right direction"* but warned it *"miss[es] a lot of content."*
- Once people reached soil/the concrete product, understanding clicked (B1: "as I scroll down, I was able to understand more").

## Section C — Recommended narrative structure (soil-first, reveal-OS)

**Confirmed:** SOIL-FIRST is correct. Sections A and B both point here — A2 (concrete problem first), A3 (abstract→physical), A6 (business model for a generalist jury), and the B1/B2/B3 evidence that the OS-first opening literally is not understood and that front-loaded verticals read as overreach. The OS / federation is the **multiplier revealed near the end as upside**, never the opening.

Design constraints carried in: ~3:00 at ~150 wpm; 5 slides; big titles, minimal text; no fabricated construction revenue (B4); end on a clear ask (A10) and answer "where does my money go?" on slide 1 (B2).

### Beat list (ordered, with rough timings)

**Beat 1 — Problem + what we do, in one breath (0:00–0:30) · Slide 1**
- Open on the concrete, painful problem: degraded/unproductive soil costs agriculture billions every year. (A1, A2)
- Immediately say who we are in one plain sentence: "Beyond Biomes brings precision medicine to soil — we read a field's living soil and deliver a custom biome that restores it." (A14)
- Answers the investor's first question instantly — money goes into the **agriculture/soil vertical**. (B2)
- *Slide:* big title, logo, names visible, one image. (B5)

**Beat 2 — The product & how it works, kept simple (0:30–1:10) · Slide 2**
- The tangible product: farmer sends a soil sample → we test it (RhizoGenie / RNA + soil chemistry reads what the microbes actually do) → we design a custom living biome → deliver it to the farmer. (A3 product beat)
- Name the differentiator simply — "we read what the microbes actually *do*, not just what's present" — that's the secret sauce. (A15)
- Explain to a five-year-old; no limitations/caveats here. (A4, A5)

**Beat 3 — Market + traction + business model (1:10–2:00) · Slide 3**
- Credibility numbers, crisp: Europe biostimulant market ~$1.8B today → $3B+ by 2030; EU regulation just opened the door. (A6, B6 — the VC/banker beat)
- Business model, factual: diagnostics from ~€300/mo; audits €5–15K; retainers €1–5K/mo recurring; local-first infra keeps costs low. (A7 — money, not features)
- Traction = the team's operator experience + early signal. (A8) Keep team to expertise, not headcount (B6).

**Beat 4 — The reveal: the method scales (the OS, as upside) (2:00–2:40) · Slide 4**
- NOW reveal the multiplier: the same method that reads soil — "eyes before muscle: observe, verify against reality, then act; never let a process grade its own work" — is a general, agentic operating system. (A3 climax)
- Position the OS/federation strictly as **future upside / why this scales cheaply**, ONE step beyond soil — not as five live verticals across three continents. (B3) Frame: "Soil is the first vertical; the same brain makes each next one faster and cheaper."
- Do NOT claim construction revenue. (B4)

**Beat 5 — The ask + close (2:40–3:00) · Slide 5**
- Concrete ask: raising [amount] to finish the soil product, scale lab testing, hire engineers. (A10)
- One-line close that ties back: "Read the living system, prove it, then scale it. Let's talk." Leaves them with a question to come ask. (A13)

### Notes for the script-builder
- Borrow the *clarity and CTA* of the soil-first script (B7) but graft on the missing content Ringo flagged: real market numbers + business model from the current OS-first script's Slides 4/6 (those beats landed and are factual).
- The OS-first script's Slides 1–3 (title "1000× operator," method-first, OS-first) are the failed structure — repurpose their content into Beat 4 (the reveal), never the opening.
- Keep verticals to "soil now, more later" — never enumerate "5 verticals / 3 continents" up front.
- Five slides, big titles, ~no body text, names + logo visible.
