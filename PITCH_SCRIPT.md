# Beyond Biomes — 3-Minute Pitch Script (soil-first, reveal-OS)

**Audience:** IDEA / Bochum ecosystem jury — Wirtschaftsentwicklung, VC, Volksbank Sprockhövel (MD), Aulinger (law firm), Bryck. Generalists who judge **the business model** (per Christiane), not the EXIST project, not team size.
**Structure rationale:** see `PITCH_RULES.md`. The OS-first / "1000× operator" opening was independently not understood by three reviewers (Ringo, Prem, Prem's wife). We open on the concrete soil problem — where the money goes — and reveal the OS as upside at the end.
**Total run-time:** ≈ 3:00 at ~150 wpm. Five beats / five light slides, big titles, minimal text.

> Delivery: warm, certain, unhurried. Land the last word of each line, then move. Pause 1 beat at every "—". Practice the FIRST line until it's automatic — it's the only hook.

---

### [0:00–0:30] · SLIDE 1 — The problem + who we are
**Say:** "Every year, farms lose billions to soil that has quietly stopped working — depleted, degraded, less alive. Beyond Biomes brings precision medicine to that soil. We read a field's living soil, then deliver a custom biome — a tailored community of beneficial microbes — that brings it back to life. This round goes into one market: agriculture."
*On screen:* logo + names visible · "Precision medicine for living soil."
*Delivery:* answer the investor's first question in the first breath — *where does the money go?* Soil.

### [0:30–1:10] · SLIDE 2 — The product, explained simply
**Say:** "Here's how it works. A farmer sends us a soil sample. In our lab we sequence it and read what the microbes are actually *doing* — not just which ones are present, but the jobs they're performing. From that, we design a living biome matched to exactly what that field is missing, and ship it back to the farmer. Reading what the soil is actually doing — that's the part no one else does."
*On screen:* sample → read → design biome → deliver (four steps, one image).
*Delivery:* explain it to a five-year-old. No caveats, no jargon. Name the secret sauce: *what the microbes do*.

### [1:10–2:00] · SLIDE 3 — Market, model & traction
**Say:** "The market is real and the timing is rare. Europe's biostimulant market is one-point-eight billion dollars today, growing past three billion by twenty-thirty — and EU regulation just opened a clear path to sell. The model is capital-efficient: a soil diagnostic starts at three hundred euros a month; audits, five to fifteen thousand; retainers, one to five thousand a month — recurring. Our infrastructure runs local-first, so costs stay low and a single operator can carry one to two million in revenue. And we're operators ourselves — with the soil science and the field experience to ship this."
*On screen:* $1.8B → $3.08B by 2030 · EU FPR 2019/1009 · €300/mo → €5–15k → €1–5k/mo · €1–2M / operator.
*Delivery:* the banker's and the VC's beat. Say the numbers crisply, then stop.

### [2:00–2:40] · SLIDE 4 — The reveal: why this scales
**Say:** "Now, the part that makes this scale. The method we use to read soil follows one rule from nature: never let a process grade its own work. DNA is proofread by a separate enzyme; the immune system checks against a reference nothing can fake. We build our software the same way — eyes before muscle: observe, verify against reality, then act. That method isn't limited to soil. It's an operating system any vertical can run on, so each new market we enter launches faster and cheaper than the last. Soil is simply the first."
*On screen:* "Eyes before muscle. Soil is the first vertical." (the OS as upside — not five live verticals).
*Delivery:* this is the lift. Slow down on *"never let a process grade its own work."* Frame the OS as pure upside, one step beyond soil.

### [2:40–3:00] · SLIDE 5 — The ask + close
**Say:** "We're raising to finish the product, scale our lab testing, and hire two engineers. Beyond Biomes: read the living system, prove it, then scale it. Let's talk."
*On screen:* the ask (switchable funding versions) · logo · QR to the live app.
*Delivery:* land the close, then silence. End with a concrete ask.
