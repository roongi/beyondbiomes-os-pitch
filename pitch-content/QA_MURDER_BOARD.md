# Beyond Biomes — Q&A Murder Board (pitch defense training)
_Comprehensive, grounded in CANONICAL_FACTS + DECISIONS. Synthesis of the lastpitchsession adversarial review + Gemini murder board + new. Answers are written to be SPOKEN: tight, confident, honest. Format: **Q** → **30-sec answer** → (bridge-back line). ⚠ = a honesty trap — never overclaim._

## How to train with this
- Drill by **category** first, then **random-fire** (have someone read Q's out of order).
- For each answer: land the **first sentence** (the reframe), give **one number**, end with a **bridge-back** to the thesis ("eyes before muscle / we fund the proof").
- Three reflexes: (1) if attacked on a claim → **concede the limit, then reframe**; (2) always convert a number into a *decision the customer makes*; (3) never say "patent" — say "patent-pending."

## 1 · BUSINESS MODEL & MONETIZATION
**Q: If the farmer gets the yield, why does the producer pay you?**
→ "Because the producer has an existential problem: up to 70% of biological products fail in the field, and a product that fails once never gets bought again. They pay us €800 a hectare not to treat the whole farm — to profile **validation plots** so their product only goes where it will work. We de-risk their product so they can sell it. We sell the eyes; they sell the muscle." (bridge: that's eyes before muscle, as a business model.)

**Q: €800/ha at ~84% margin — isn't that just expensive sequencing?**
→ "DNA sequencing tells you *who's there* — a guest list. We sell the *decision*: what will actually work in this field, closed to a tailored product. The margin is software-like because compute is local-first — about €30–60 a month — and it compounds: every paid field improves the model the next producer buys." ⚠ say margin as **target**.

**Q: Farmers are notoriously hard to sell to — how do you scale?**
→ "We don't sell farm-by-farm. Our payer is the **producer** — a concentrated B2B market of dozens, not millions. One producer contract carries many hectares. The farmer is the beneficiary, not the checkout."

## 2 · TRACTION & CLIENTS
**Q: You say 120 B2B deals — where's the revenue?**
→ "Total transparency: we're pre-revenue, finishing TRL 5. The **120 deals are María Paz's verified track record** — it's *why* she's on the team; it let us skip cold outreach and land USACH in Chile. We have **five signed LOIs**, including Dr. Cyro Cavalcante, a biofertilizer producer in 35+ countries who signed to white-label through us." ⚠ LOIs = intent, not booked revenue.

**Q: Are the LOIs binding?**
→ "They're signed letters of intent — design-partner commitments for pilot access and data rights across three climate zones, for almost no cash. We represent them as channel validation, not revenue."

**Q: Why Chile if you're EXIST-funded in Bochum?**
→ "Biology dictates our speed. In Europe we get one harvest a year to train the AI. Chile gives us the **counter-season** — while Europe freezes, our model trains on live summer fields. It roughly doubles our iteration speed. That's a capital-efficiency multiplier, not a distraction."

## 3 · SCIENCE & TECH
**Q: What's actually different — everyone reads soil DNA.**
→ "DNA is the guest list — who's present. We read **function** — who's actually working: nutrient cycling, stress response, pathogen suppression. It's the difference between a guest list and the live runtime. That's what lets us *prescribe*, not just describe." ⚠ function via functional metagenomics/RNA-seq; don't claim it's the only method.

**Q: R² 0.68 isn't production-grade. Why should we believe 0.85?**
→ "We're radically honest: 0.68 is our demonstrator today, 0.81 on the EMP500 benchmark. This round funds the exact **625-metagenome trial** across Germany, Chile and Spain to reach 0.85. Even 0.78 is commercially useful — the model degrades gracefully. We fund the proof; we don't assume it."

**Q: If functional reading is so good, why don't Biome Makers / Trace Genomics do it?**
→ "Paradigm and cost. They're built on the DNA 'guest list' and a cloud cost base. Function generates far heavier data; on their architecture the hyperscaler bill breaks the unit economics. Our moat is our CTO's **local-first compute** that processes it at the edge affordably — plus a data flywheel that compounds on fields they never saw."

**Q: Is the platform real or slideware?**
→ "It's live at review.beyondbiomes.de — 221 trained models, ~890 API endpoints, 15,000+ real samples, crop-disease detection at 97%, built on 696 commits. Scan the QR and run a diagnosis yourself in 30 seconds."

## 4 · MARKET & TAM
**Q: Your TAM numbers jump around — 12.5B, 3.5B?**
→ "Two honest numbers: the broad European biostimulant/soil market is around **€12.5 billion**; our *serviceable* intersection bottoms-up to **~€3.5 billion**, with a €150M SAM and a €15M SOM by year five at ~375 customers. We pitch the serviceable one to investors who do diligence." 

**Q: +20–25% yield — is that real?**
→ "We say **target**. The peer-reviewed field average is ~18%; commercial products average ~14%. Our edge is *targeting* — putting the right consortium where it works. We'll never put an unqualified 25% in a farmer contract." 

**Q: €12B EU crop loss — source?**
→ "That's our estimate of blind biostimulant/fertilizer mis-application — say 'around twelve billion.' The named figures run higher: the EU JRC puts erosion alone at €1.25B/yr, EEA puts unhealthy-soil cost at €40–73B/yr." ⚠ never state €12B as a hard sourced fact.

## 5 · MOAT & DEFENSIBILITY
**Q: What stops a giant from copying this?**
→ "Three things: a **data flywheel** — every paid field sharpens predictions, so fast-followers start from zero; a **45–66-month / €1.3M replication barrier** to rebuild the dataset; and **regulation** — EU 2019/1009 approves only four microorganism types, excluding 88% of makers, and forces producers to *evidence* claims, which is exactly what we sell."

**Q: Your patent position?**
→ "**Patent-pending** — first filing now with the DPMA, PCT/EP at seed, on the Siderophore-Surfactin synergy. We analyzed 150+ patent families — no blockers. The real moat is the combination plus the data, not one molecule." ⚠ NEVER say 'granted patent.'

**Q: The 17-agent pipeline is a trade secret — what if it leaks?**
→ "It's local-first by design — pipeline and training data never leave our control; NDAs with pilot partners; and the flywheel makes the *output*, prediction accuracy, the defensible asset even if methods leak."

**Q: What exactly does €1.5M buy, and when are you cash-flow positive?**
→ "It's a milestone raise to TRL 7, not a path to profit. 40% producer pilots, 30% the 625-metagenome validation, 20% go-to-market, 10% regulatory. Break-even is modeled at month 36–42 on the base case; this round de-risks the Series A." 

**Q: Your dilution / funding path?**
→ "Seed €1.5M at €8–10M pre, then Series A €3M, Series B €5M — about €10.5M cumulative; founders retain ~48% through B. Plausible exit €30–50M to BASF, Bayer, Corteva or dsm-firmenich over ~10 years." ⚠ projections.

**Q: What if the validation trial misses 0.85?**
→ "It degrades gracefully — 0.78 still validates products commercially. We'd re-scope consortium claims, not the company. We're funding the proof precisely so this isn't a binary bet."

## 7 · REGULATORY
**Q: EU 2019/1009 limits microbial biostimulants to four taxa (CMC 7) — doesn't that cap you?**
→ "Crucially, **we don't sell the microbes — we sell the diagnostic and the design.** Our customer, the producer, owns their product's registration. Where we recommend consortia we work within CMC 7 today and track EBIC's push to widen the list. The regulation is a *tailwind*: it forces producers to evidence claims — our exact product."
