1. The opening is strong, but “what a time to be alive” is less specific than the sharper “What if…” bookend; IDEA jurors will remember a framed question better than a mood line.

2. Slide 4 risks overclaiming “doctor for the soil” with “diagnose, prescribe, and treat.” Better: diagnose and prescribe; producers deliver the treatment. That protects the business model: “we sell the eyes; they sell the muscle.”

3. The script says “fifteen thousand samples,” while canonical facts now support 15,231 or roughly 15,000. Use “over fifteen thousand” to avoid brittle precision.

4. The OpenAPI story is missing from the spoken pitch. For VC credibility, say “around 890 working endpoints today; formal OpenAPI contract next” so platform reality and extensibility both land.

5. The validation slide says “six-hundred-metagenome” but the locked ask funds a 625-metagenome trial. Use 625 consistently, and keep R² 0.85 clearly as a target.

6. The market is absent. A 3-minute investor pitch needs at least one compact market line: €12.5B broad market, ~€3.5B serviceable intersection, without turning the story into spreadsheet theatre.

**IMPROVED SCRIPT**

**[SLIDE 1 · TITLE]**  
“What if we have been trying to fix the world’s most critical living system using a dead language? I’m Dr. Ringo Schwabe, founder of Beyond Biomes. In AI, everyone is racing for stronger models. But Gartner expects over forty percent of agentic AI projects to be cancelled by 2027, not because the models are weak, but because they are blind. Our rule is simple: eyes before muscle. Read the system before you change it.”

**[SLIDE 2 · PROBLEM]**  
“Start with soil. Agriculture still reads living microbiomes as flat chemistry: nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium, pH. That is like debugging software by weighing the hardware. It tells you what is there. It does not tell you what is missing, failing, or ready to work.”

**[SLIDE 3 · LANDSCAPE]**  
“So three people stay disconnected. The farmer knows the field by hand, but cannot see the biology underneath. The scientist understands the failure, but is far from the field. The producer sells the biological product, and hopes it survives.”

**[SLIDE 4 · TECHNOLOGY]**  
“Beyond Biomes puts them into one loop. The farmer sends soil. Our platform reads not just the guest list of who is there, but the live runtime: what microbes are actually doing. Today that means seventeen AI agents, 221 models, over fifteen thousand samples, and around 890 working endpoints. The formal OpenAPI contract comes next.”

**[SLIDE 5 · BRIDGE]**  
“This matters because sixty to seventy percent of single strains die in the field. A lab microbe dropped into drought, salt, heat, or chemical stress enters a biological warzone. We measure the battlefield first, then design the consortium built to survive it. Our published greenhouse strains lifted manganese by 396 percent and phosphorus by 309 percent. Field proof is what this seed round funds.”

**[SLIDE 6 · BUSINESS MODEL]**  
“The split is clean. The farmer gets the benefit: a target of twenty to twenty-five percent more yield with thirty percent less fertiliser. The producer pays us to validate, tailor, and prove the product. We sell the eyes; they sell the muscle.”

**[SLIDE 7 · TRACTION]**  
“This is not a slideware platform. We have five signed letters of intent, including a 1,200-hectare German cooperative, and Dr. Cyro Cavalcante, whose biofertilizer business sells in more than thirty-five countries, has signed to white-label through us. Maria Paz also brings a personal track record of more than 120 B2B agri-deals in Latin America.”

**[SLIDE 8 · COUNTER-SEASON]**  
“And Chile gives us an honest speed advantage. When Europe freezes, our AI trains on live summer fields in Chile, then returns that learning to Germany and Spain. We retrain every six months, not once a year.”

**[SLIDE 9 · SCALE]**  
“Soil is the first system. Underneath is our agentic layer: 1,505 commits of infrastructure that already built more than four verticals, from lab science to construction. Soil is the first machine off the line. The factory is the bigger company.”

**[SLIDE 10 · TEAM + MARKET]**  
“The team is built for this: the inventor of the method, a Nature Communications lead author, the engineer who built the platform, and an operator with deep LATAM agri-sales experience. The market is large enough twice: €12.5 billion broad biostimulants and soil, and about €3.5 billion serviceable intersection.”

**[SLIDE 11 · VALIDATION]**  
“We are honest about proof. Patent-pending, not granted. Greenhouse proof today, field proof next. Our model is R-squared 0.68 demonstrated, 0.81 benchmarked, and the 625-metagenome Germany-Chile-Spain trial funds the path to a 0.85 field target. We fund the proof. We do not assume it.”

**[SLIDE 12 · ASK]**  
“So the ask is €1.5 million seed: to buy an industry its eyes. What if we finally stopped guessing? Read the system before you change it. Thank you.”
